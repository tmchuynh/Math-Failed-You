Why Are You "Bad" at Math?

But anxiety alone is not the "silent" killer. The stronger mechanism is math engagement.

The Body Responds to Language, Not Logic

fMRI scans demonstrate that for highly math-anxious individuals, the anticipation of performing mathematics activates the brain's pain network, leading to a fight-or-flight response that impairs working memory and mathematical performance.

Anxiety Doesn't Equate to Poor Performance

Difficulties in mathematics are often rooted in linguistic processing rather than mathematical reasoning. Kirshner's early work established that language proficiency and mathematical proficiency are separable constructs - you can have one without the other.

The Core Problem: It's a Language Barrier

Key Takeaway
Most people who say
"I'm bad at math" are actually saying "vocabulary and notation deters me."

The ability is already there.
The DELIVERY is the problem.

Unfamiliar or dense notation increases cognitive load, which exacerbates anxiety. When notation does not align with a learner's intuitive understanding, performance drops even when conceptual comprehension is intact

Emily Rada and Anne M. Lucietto Math Anxiety - A Literature Review on Confounding Factors (1978)

Thinking is a form of communication. Students "hit a wall" not because they can't do the math, but because they can't parse the formal language it's written in.

Anna Sfard Thinking as Communicating (2008)

Notation alone is a significant barrier when not coordinated with concrete, intuitive representations. Integrating semiotic representations (symbols, graphs, algebra) with real-world framing is critical for conceptual understanding.

Mathematics is not a culture-free universal code delivered in one official register; rather, it is a set of human practices that develops in every society through counting, measuring, designing, locating, trading, and explaining relationships in culturally meaningful ways, which is a pattern that remains visible today in many everyday settings

\(K\): # of winning balls drawn.

\(k\): # of your balls that must match the winning balls.
