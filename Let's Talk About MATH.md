## But First, Let's Examine Why You THINK You're "Bad" At Math

64% of people report having math anxiety. 

And the effects are HUGE.

As mentioned, this barrier tracks into a path which locks you out of the fastest-growing fields, which was proven by Özdemir [Urz-deh-meer] in 2025 and studied by Ferdinand [FUR-di-nand] in 2024.

When your body experiences math anxiety, your body's stress response mimics the same response to a real physical threat and imparis working memory during tasks heavy in symbolic or notational content, which causes a feedback loop, documented by Ashcraft and Krause [KROW-zuh]. Lyons and Beilock further studied this using fMRI scans in 2012.

But, as I will prove, and Kirshner [KERSH-nur] already did in 1985, math anxiety isn't correlated to poor performance. 

## It's a Language Barrier

There are countless of studies, published journals, and research around this subject specifically that support this reality. The conceputal understanding exists. Jargon and complicated notation is what deters students from the subject.

When students, professors, everyone in a professional field who claims they are bad at math, they are really saying that the vocabulary and notation is what sets them apart from those who have mastered the language.

## Math You Already Use Daily

Here are some more examples of what YOU call advanced math that you perform on a regular basis.

### Real Analysis

You adjust your thermostat all the time, especially these days with the weather not being able to make up its mind whether it wants to be hot or cold.

### Linear Optimization

When shopping, you always (hopefully) want to save your hard earned money and compare unit prices to find the best deals.

### Chaos Theory

You know if you wake up just ten minutes late, it might end up with you leaving the house later and hitting traffic you usually avoid getting to work or school, which leads to a whole bunch of unneeded stress.

### Knot Theory

You apply this, or used to before everything became wireless - so maybe your generation haven't even experienced this struggle, when you untangled your headphones you stuffed in your pockets or in your bag.

### Set Theory

Going back to shopping, when you use those filters. You're applying set theory.

### Universal Quantifiers

You do this nearly everyday when you say things like "Everyone failed that chemistry test" or when your professor says"Everyone in this class will get 20pts extra credit". Yeah? Just a suggestion.
